## 3. Gallery & channel  [p.23–28]

| Property | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Longitudinal fall (gradient) | **0.1 %** (10 cm per 100 m), constant P9 → P-5, except two steps | [p.26] |
| Stepped fall under P6 | **1.2 m** (reserve for level adjustment) | [p.26–27] |
| Fall under P4 | **1 m** (drops below the limestone band) | [p.27] |
| Channel starts | **≈13 m west of P9** (no channel at the eastern start) | [p.28] |
| Channel cross-section | height ≈ **45 cm**; width **12 – 15 cm** | [p.28] |
| Water flow height | **5 – 7 cm** | [p.28] |
| Cover slabs | 6–20 cm thick, 25–50 cm long; sandstone ballast up to **60 cm** | [p.28] |
| Construction | **dry masonry, no mortar** anywhere | [p.28] |
| Channel floor level | coincides with the **permeable sandstone / impermeable marl boundary** | [p.28, fig.5-24] |
| Largest survey deviation | **>3 m** between P4 and P5 | [p.26] |
| Counter-excavation | only P7↔P8 (½-gallery-width meeting error) | [p.26] |

**Gallery wall/crown by section** [p.23, fig.5-15]:
- **P9 → P6:** entirely in solid rock; section **rectangular** with rounded corners.
- **P6 → P5:** intersects marl; **trapezoidal**, widening.
- **P5 → P4:** limestone walls, marl crown; **wider** (blocks removed).
- **P4 → P0:** limestone crown; downstream of P4 the profile becomes **triangular** (tip down).
- **P0 → NW:** entirely in marl; **backfilled with spoil** (unstable).

## 4. Geology / stratigraphy  [p.6, p.14–15, fig.4-2/4-3]

Strata, top → bottom (legend codes from fig.4-3):

| Unit | Code | Description | Role | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Scree / weathered rock, soft ground | — | Slope deposits, ~2 m (up to 10 m in Haedchen) | cover | [p.15, p.18] |
| **Luxembourg sandstone (Lias)** | **li²** | Thickness **30–80 m** (upper ~20 m weathered), at **350–400 m a.s.l.** | **main aquifer** (permeable) | [p.6, p.14] |
| Sandy marl, marl & limestone beds (Lias) | **li¹** | blue-grey marl + grey limestone | aquitard/mixed | [fig.4-3] |
| Mudstone & grey conglomeratic sandstone (Keuper) | **ko** | red mudstone | impermeable | [fig.4-3] |
| Dolomitic marl & fine dolomite beds (Keuper) | **km³** | basal unit | impermeable | [fig.4-3] |

- Water percolates the sandstone and **accumulates at the sandstone/marl
  interface**; spring horizons are at the **base of the sandstone**. [p.6, p.14]
- Strata **dip ~2 % toward the southeast**; natural drainage is toward the
  Haedchen depression — the qanat drains **westward, contrary** to this. [p.14, p.15]
- The **Pëtschend** is a **central horst** bounded N & S by ~E–W faults where
  strata are vertically realigned; some groundwater leaks along faults to the
  Dauvebur (N) and Op der Rëll (S) springs. [p.14, fig.4-3]
- Pëtschend spur ≈ **395 m a.s.l.**; Haedchen (marshy source area) ≈ **375 m
  a.s.l.**, ~**600 m** apart. [p.7]

## 8. Implications for the 3D model

- **Gallery is near-level at ~0.1 %** (not a visible slope) — depth variation
  comes from surface topography, with **two steps** (1.2 m at P6, 1 m at P4). [p.26–27]
- **Documented shaft depths** now available: P1 = 30 m, P3 = 35 m, P5 = 36 m,
  P-4 = 12 m, P-5A = 8 m, P-7A ≈ 6 m. [p.9,16–22]
- **Channel floor = sandstone/marl boundary**; groundwater flows E along it,
  qanat channel flows W below. [p.28, fig.5-24]
- **P-4 overflow weir** (>12 cm → lateral channel) and **P-7A drop-shaft** are
  distinctive construction details. [p.20, p.22]
- **P9 = mother shaft**; no channel for the first ~13 m from the east. [p.19, p.28]
